
# RuntimeBrain Principles

## Why Principles Exist

Vision defines where we are going.

Tenets define what we believe.

Principles define how we make decisions.

These principles guide product development, engineering tradeoffs, architecture choices, and company strategy.

When faced with uncertainty, we use these principles to determine the best path forward.

---

# 1. Documentation Before Code

We design before we build.

Major features should be described, reviewed, and understood before implementation begins.

Clear thinking produces better software.

---

# 2. Explain Before Optimize

Optimization without understanding creates distrust.

RuntimeBrain should always explain decisions before attempting to improve them.

Visibility is the foundation of optimization.

---

# 3. Evidence Before Automation

Every recommendation should be supported by evidence.

Every automated action should be traceable to a clear rationale.

Users should understand why a decision was made.

---

# 4. Developer Trust Above Convenience

Trust is more valuable than short-term adoption.

We will not sacrifice transparency, predictability, or user control for convenience.

When in doubt, choose the option that strengthens trust.

---

# 5. Incremental Adoption Wins

Developers should not need to redesign their systems to benefit from RuntimeBrain.

The best integrations require minimal effort and provide immediate value.

Adoption should be progressive.

---

# 6. Interfaces Before Implementations

Interfaces outlive implementations.

We invest heavily in stable interfaces and evolve implementations behind them.

Good interfaces create flexibility and longevity.

---

# 7. Vendor Neutrality By Default

RuntimeBrain serves user outcomes, not provider interests.

Our architecture, recommendations, and product decisions should remain independent of any single model, provider, or cloud.

---

# 8. Earn Adoption Before Expansion

RuntimeBrain should earn the right to become more deeply integrated.

Every product should provide value before requesting additional permissions, infrastructure, or operational commitment.

Trust and adoption are earned incrementally.

---

# 9. Measure Outcomes, Not Activity

The goal is not more AI requests.

The goal is better AI decisions.

We evaluate success based on outcomes such as quality, efficiency, reliability, governance, and user value.

---

# 10. Build for the Ecosystem We Expect

The AI ecosystem will continue to evolve rapidly.

We design systems that adapt to new providers, models, architectures, and workflows without requiring fundamental redesign.

We build for the future, not just today's landscape.

---

# Final Principle

Whenever a decision is difficult, ask:

"Does this help organizations continuously improve AI decisions?"

If the answer is yes, we are likely moving in the right direction.
